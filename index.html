About the Data

EBCI PHHS monitors Escherichia coli (E. coli) as an indicator of fecal contamination in rivers and streams used for recreation. Results are reported as colony‑forming units per 100 mL (CFU/100 mL) or most probable number (MPN/100 mL) depending on the method.

  • How to interpret results — The EPA’s 2012 Recreational Water Quality Criteria use two benchmarks for freshwater E. coli: a 30‑day geometric mean ≤ 126 CFU/100 mL and a single‑sample Statistical Threshold Value ≤ 410 CFU/100 mL. We flag “Advisory” when the latest value is ≥ 410 or the 30‑day GM ≥ 126. A “Caution” band (235–409) is shown for added context.
  • Sampling & methods — Grab samples are collected using sterile bottles at wadeable locations and analyzed using EPA‑approved methods (e.g., Colilert‑18/Quanti‑Tray or EPA Method 1603). Results typically represent conditions at the time of sampling and can change rapidly after rainfall.
  • Public health guidance (CDC) — Avoid swallowing water; keep children from putting hands or objects in their mouths; wash hands or use sanitizer before eating; avoid water for 24–48 hours after heavy rain; stay out if you’re sick with diarrhea; and keep cuts or wounds out of the water. Learn more: https://www.cdc.gov/healthy-swimming/prevention/index.html and https://www.cdc.gov/ecoli/prevention.html
  • Data notes — Non‑detects (“<”) are treated as half the detection limit for geometric means. Values above the upper reading range (“>”) are displayed as reported. Locations without coordinates will appear in the table and chart but not on the map.
